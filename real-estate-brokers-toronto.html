Toronto Real Estate: What You Need to Know

The Greater Toronto Area is one of North America's most active housing markets. Here's what buyers and sellers should understand before engaging a broker:

Buying in Toronto

Demand consistently outpaces supply in most GTA neighbourhoods. Pre-approval for a mortgage before starting your search is strongly recommended. A buyer's agent works at no direct cost to you — their commission is paid by the seller's proceeds.

Selling in Toronto

Pricing strategy, staging, and listing timing are critical in the GTA. An experienced listing broker will provide a Comparative Market Analysis (CMA) to position your home competitively. Commission rates are negotiable but typically range from 3.5% to 5% of the sale price.

Investing in Toronto Real Estate

Toronto condos, multi-unit properties, and pre-construction investments each carry different risk profiles. Work with a broker who specializes in investment properties and understands rental regulations under Ontario's Residential Tenancies Act.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I verify a real estate broker is licensed in Ontario?

You can search the RECO public registry at reco.on.ca to confirm any agent or broker holds a valid Ontario licence.

What's the difference between a real estate agent and a broker in Ontario?

A broker has completed additional education and experience requirements beyond the basic salesperson licence. A brokerage (the company) must be run by a broker of record. Both can legally represent buyers and sellers.

Do I need a real estate broker to buy a home in Toronto?

You are not legally required to use a broker, but it is strongly recommended. Transactions in the GTA are complex, fast-moving, and high-value — professional representation protects your interests at every stage.

Are real estate commissions negotiable in Ontario?

Yes. Commission rates are not set by law and are negotiable between the seller and their listing broker. Most GTA transactions involve total commission of 3.5%–5% split between buyer and seller representation.
